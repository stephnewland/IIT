Ideal Job

I am not one hundred percent certain on the direction I want to head in the world of IT, however, there has been an area of interest that has always appealed to me throughout my studies and personal research within the IT industry. Despite being heavily stereotyped through its representation in the media, the field of cyber security has continually been a fascination and a direction I have looked into. Both roles I found for this assignment that I thought might be a potential career path were also within this area and are the positions of Cyber Security Analyst and Penetration Tester.

Job Title

Cyber Security Analyst (sometimes referred to as Information Security Analysts)
Via Deloitte Australia

Deloitte Snapshot

Description

As a Cyber Security Analyst, the role involves the planning, organisation and operating of security measures that are established to secure and protect a company’s network, system, and assets. They facilitate teamwork, working with analysts in the Deloitte’s Global Threat Intelligence team around the world to ensure any unauthorised network activity, compromisations or breaches are managed accordingly.

Written analytic reports and regular briefings will be used to communicate and discuss findings with superiors and stakeholders to keep ever yone informed of any potential information exposures. Other responsibilities included in this role are creating contingency plans, installing firewall and encryption tools, researching trends in security and IT-related areas, and educating other company members on security topics and concerns.

Learning Plan

I would begin by completing my degree through RMIT, as well as undertaking independent study through Udemy, Skillshare, or Coursera to build upon current knowledge and develop new skills that may be useful in the future. From there, I would simultaneously work on the further development of my current skills in Java and Python. PowerShell has also been mentioned in quite a few job advertisements I have seen for Cyber Security Analysts so it may be a good program to begin to learn over the next year or two.

Then, further development of my understanding of computer networking would be worked on, before the commencement of study on database security and malware detection software (of which may be covered in future courses, or in a future degree structure with RMIT or another provider, but it is always good to be prepared). I would then look into a Bachelor’s Degree in Cyber Security and then into any relevant certificates as required (Risk Management, Penetration Testing, CompTIA A+ certification).
